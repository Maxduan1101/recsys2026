Best non-ranking idea, but still not worth a final artifact change unless it only rejects obviously broken responses.

Risk:

- selecting per-row A/B responses can overfit local Distinct-2
- selector may prefer compact/mechanical text
- public judge=1.0 says you need better explanation, but not necessarily more lexical variety
- any new selector bug can silently create unnatural responses

Use response QA only as an audit, not as a new blender.

Allowed:

flag bad/noisy/hallucinated responses
manually inspect worst 30
fall back only if response is objectively invalid

Not allowed now:

train or tune a per-row response selector
hash-based A/B blending
Distinct-2-maximizing style router
2. Existing-prediction rank gating

Too dangerous now.

You already tried many near-miss gates. A tiny local nDCG delta does not justify touching the ranking when public feedback has only 80 rows.

Reject unless:

same top-20 ranking on Blind A
or only changes rows with hard, pre-declared safety evidence

At this point, rank gating is more likely to produce “one clever exception too many” than a real gain.

Stop.

Embedding probes already hurt first-fold / validation. Reviving them now means new features, new calibration, new failure modes, and likely top-rank churn. That is not final-round work.
